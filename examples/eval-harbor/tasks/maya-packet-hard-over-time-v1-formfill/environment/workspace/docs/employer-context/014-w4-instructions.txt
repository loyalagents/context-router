Title: How to Complete Your Form W-4 for Federal Tax Withholding

Purpose
----------------------------------------------------------------------
This document provides instructions on how to complete Form W-4, Employee's Withholding Certificate. The W-4 tells the company how much federal income tax to withhold from your pay. It is important to complete this form accurately to avoid having too much or too little tax withheld.

You will complete your Form W-4 electronically in LedgerPay, our payroll system. These instructions are for federal withholding only. State tax withholding forms are completed separately, if applicable.

Completing the Form W-4
----------------------------------------------------------------------
The electronic Form W-4 in LedgerPay mirrors the official IRS form. It is divided into five steps. Steps 1 and 5 are required for all employees. Steps 2, 3, and 4 are optional but will help ensure your withholding is accurate for your personal financial situation.

Step 1: Enter Personal Information
This step includes your name, address, Social Security number, and filing status. Your name, address, and SSN will be pre-populated in LedgerPay based on the information you provided during onboarding. Please verify that it is correct.

You must select exactly one filing status from the options shown in the
employee portal. This knowledge-base article describes the workflow and does
not record any employee's selected option.

Choose the filing status you expect to use when you file your annual tax return.
If your situation is unclear, contact a tax professional before submitting.

Step 2: Multiple Jobs or Spouse Works
Complete this step only if you (1) hold more than one job at a time or (2) have
a spouse who also works and use the relevant joint-return option. If this does
not apply to you, you may skip this step.

The form provides three options to calculate your withholding across multiple jobs. You only need to use one method, and it should only be done on the W-4 for ONE of your jobs (typically the highest paying one).

  (a) Use the IRS's Tax Withholding Estimator at www.irs.gov/W4App. This is the most accurate method.
  (b) Use the Multiple Jobs Worksheet on page 3 of the IRS Form W-4 and enter the result in Step 4(c).
  (c) Check the box in Step 2(c) if there are only two jobs total for your household and they have similar pay.

If you do not account for multiple household incomes, you will likely have too little tax withheld.

Step 3: Claim Dependents
If you have dependents, you can claim them here to reduce your withholding.

  - Qualifying Children: Multiply the number of qualifying children under age 17 by $2,000.
  - Other Dependents: Multiply the number of other dependents by $500.

Enter the total dollar amount on the line for Step 3. LedgerPay will perform the calculation for you.

Step 4: Other Adjustments (Optional)
This step is for other financial situations that affect your tax liability.

  (a) Other Income (not from jobs): Enter any other estimated income you expect for the year that won't have withholding (e.g., interest, dividends, retirement income).
  (b) Deductions: If you expect to claim deductions other than the standard deduction, use the Deductions Worksheet on page 3 of the IRS Form W-4 and enter the result here.
  (c) Extra Withholding: Enter any additional amount of tax you want withheld from each paycheck.

How to Submit in LedgerPay
----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Log in to your LedgerPay employee portal.
2. Navigate to: My Profile > Payroll > Tax Withholding.
3. Select "Federal Withholding" to open the electronic Form W-4.
4. Complete the steps as described above. The system will guide you.
5. Review your entries and provide your electronic signature to submit the form.

Changes take effect within 1-2 pay cycles.

Reviewing and Updating Your W-4
----------------------------------------------------------------------
You can update your W-4 at any time during the year if your personal or financial situation changes (e.g., marriage, birth of a child, change in income). It is recommended to perform a "paycheck checkup" at least once a year using the IRS Tax Withholding Estimator to ensure your withholding is on track.
