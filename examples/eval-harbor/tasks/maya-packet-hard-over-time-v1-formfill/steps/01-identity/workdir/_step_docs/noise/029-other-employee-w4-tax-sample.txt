Title: Example: How to Complete Your Form W-4

This article provides a detailed example of a completed Form W-4, Employee's Withholding Certificate. Use this guide to help you understand how to fill out your own W-4 in LedgerPay.

IMPORTANT: This is a sample form for a fictional employee. The values shown are for illustrative purposes only. Do not copy these values directly. Your personal and financial situation will determine the correct entries for your own form. Consult a tax professional for personalized advice.

--- EXAMPLE FORM W-4 ---

Form W-4 (2026)
Employee’s Withholding Certificate
Department of the Treasury
Internal Revenue Service

(a) First name and middle initial: John A.
    Last name: Sample
    Address: 123 Example Lane
    City or town, state, and ZIP code: Anytown, CA 94000

(b) Social security number: ***-**-6789

(c) Filing Status (check only one box):
    [ ] Option A
    [X] Option B
    [ ] Option C

Complete Steps 2–4 ONLY if they apply to you; otherwise, skip to Step 5. See page 2 for more information on each step, who can claim exemption from withholding, when to use the online estimator, and privacy.

Step 2: Multiple Jobs or Spouse Works
Complete this step if you (1) hold more than one job at a time, or (2) have a
spouse who also works and use the relevant joint-return option. The correct
amount of withholding depends on the income earned from all of these jobs.

Do only one of the following:
(a) Use the estimator at www.irs.gov/W4App for most accurate withholding; or
(b) Use the Multiple Jobs Worksheet on page 3 and enter the result in Step 4(c) below for roughly accurate withholding; or
(c) If there are only two jobs in your household, you may check this box. Do the same on Form W-4 for the other job. This option is accurate for jobs with similar pay; otherwise, more tax than necessary may be withheld. [ ]

TIP: For the highest accuracy, we recommend using the IRS Tax Withholding Estimator.

Step 3: Claim Dependents
If your total income is within the threshold for your selected status:
Multiply the number of qualifying children under age 17 by $2,000 -> $ 2000
Multiply the number of other dependents by $500 . . . . . . . -> $  500
Add the amounts above and enter the total here . . . . . . . . . . 3. $ 2500

Step 4: Other Adjustments (optional)
(a) Other income (not from jobs). If you want tax withheld for other
    income you expect this year that won’t have withholding, such as
    interest, dividends, and retirement income, enter the amount of
    other income here. This is not your taxed income. . . . . . . . . 4(a) $

(b) Deductions. If you expect to claim deductions other than the
    standard deduction and want to reduce your withholding, use the
    Deductions Worksheet on page 3 and enter the result here. . . . . 4(b) $ 8000

(c) Extra withholding. Enter any additional tax you want withheld
    each pay period. . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . 4(c) $ 25.00

--- END OF EXAMPLE FORM ---

Instructions for LedgerPay:
1. Log in to the LedgerPay employee portal.
2. Navigate to "My Pay" > "Tax Withholding".
3. Click "Update W-4".
4. The system will guide you through a wizard that corresponds to the steps on the paper form.
5. Enter your personal information in Step 1.
6. If Step 2 applies to you, use the IRS estimator and enter the resulting values into the LedgerPay wizard.
7. Enter any dependent amounts from Step 3.
8. Enter any optional adjustments from Step 4.
9. Review your entries and provide your electronic signature to submit the form.

Your changes will typically be reflected in the next payroll cycle after they are processed. You can update your W-4 at any time during the year if your personal or financial situation changes.
